Funding Sources for Community Land Trusts (CLTs)

Federal Government Programs

HUD HOME Investment Partnerships Program

Description: Provides grants to states and localities for affordable housing projects.

Focus: CLTs can use HOME funds for building, purchasing, or rehabilitating affordable housing.

More Information

HUD Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) Program

Description: Offers flexible funding for community development needs, including affordable housing.

Focus: Funds can be used for property acquisition, public improvements, and more.

More Information

USDA Rural Development Grants and Loans

Description: Supports housing and economic development in rural areas.

Focus: CLTs in rural locations can use these grants for housing projects and land acquisition.

More Information

Federal Home Loan Bank (FHLB) Affordable Housing Program

Description: Provides grants to support affordable housing initiatives.

Focus: CLTs can apply through partner banks to secure funds for affordable housing projects.

More Information

Low-Income Housing Tax Credits (LIHTC)

Description: Provides tax credits for private developers building affordable housing.

Focus: CLTs may partner with developers utilizing LIHTC to create affordable units.

More Information

State and Local Government Programs

State Housing Trust Funds

Description: Many states have housing trust funds that allocate resources to affordable housing projects.

Focus: These funds can often be used by CLTs for land acquisition or housing construction.

Example Source by State

Local Community Development Funds

Description: Some cities and counties have funds for community development, including affordable housing.

Focus: CLTs can apply for these funds to support local affordable housing initiatives.

State Housing Finance Agencies (HFA) Programs

Description: HFAs often provide financing and grants for affordable housing.

Focus: Programs vary by state but may include grants or loans for property acquisition.

Philanthropic Foundations and Nonprofits

Ford Foundation

Description: Funds initiatives focused on social justice, including affordable housing.

Focus: Supports programs that promote economic security and affordable housing.

More Information

Kresge Foundation

Description: Supports initiatives in arts, health, and human services, including affordable housing.

Focus: Provides grants and low-interest loans for housing projects.

More Information

MacArthur Foundation

Description: Focuses on housing initiatives that address economic and social equity.

Focus: Provides grants to support innovative affordable housing models.

More Information

Grounded Solutions Network

Description: Offers technical assistance, training, and funding resources specifically for CLTs.

Focus: Helps CLTs with capacity building, funding, and policy support.

More Information

Robert Wood Johnson Foundation

Description: Focuses on health, but includes initiatives that address affordable housing and community well-being.

Focus: Supports projects that improve the social determinants of health, including housing.

More Information

Private Sector and Impact Investment Sources

Enterprise Community Partners

Description: Provides loans, tax credits, and grants for affordable housing.

Focus: Works with CLTs and affordable housing developers to secure funding.

More Information

LISC (Local Initiatives Support Corporation)

Description: Offers loans, grants, and technical assistance to support affordable housing.

Focus: Works with CLTs on community development and affordable housing projects.

More Information

Impact Investing Funds

Description: Funds focused on social returns, such as Calvert Impact Capital and the ImpactAssets Community Investment Fund.

Focus: Impact investors often support affordable housing and sustainable community projects.

Bank of America Community Development Banking

Description: Provides lending and investment solutions for affordable housing.

Focus: Works with nonprofits and CLTs to finance affordable housing projects.

More Information

These funding sources provide a solid foundation for CLTs seeking resources to acquire and develop affordable housing properties. Access to diverse funding—government, philanthropic, and private—is crucial for CLTs to grow their impact and secure sustainable financing.
